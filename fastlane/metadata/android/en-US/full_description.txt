BLE Tripwire turns your phone into the hub of a passive, camera-free perimeter
alert system.

Inexpensive ESP32-S3 sensor nodes passively listen for Bluetooth Low Energy
advertisements (phones, wearables, trackers) and report sightings to your phone
over its Wi-Fi hotspot. Everything runs on the phone itself — no cloud, no
account, no external server, no camera.

Features:

• Passive only — the sensors never transmit scan requests or connect to
  anything; they just listen.
• Privacy by design — device addresses are anonymized on ingest with a
  per-install random HMAC key. Raw identifiers are never needed for alerts.
• Zone-breach state machine — deterministic WATCH / ALERT / CLOSING states,
  including detection of emitters that are approaching (rising signal
  strength across sweeps).
• Baseline capture — teach it your own devices once; after that only
  unexpected emitters raise alerts.
• Tamper-evident audit chain — every alert and state change is written to an
  HMAC-chained log you can verify for integrity, useful as an incident record.
• Data-sufficiency gate — the app refuses to produce a confident assessment
  from thin or stale data instead of guessing.
• Fully deterministic — no AI/ML layer, no network calls, all logic is
  readable in the source.

Honest limitations: RSSI is not distance, a single sensor cannot triangulate,
and modern phones randomize their Bluetooth addresses — this is a presence and
approach tripwire, not a tracker or a positioning system.

Requires: one or more ESP32-S3 boards flashed with the included open-source
firmware (Arduino sketches in the repository), joined to your phone's hotspot.
